About

What I do.

My dream job title is optimizer. Nothing brings me more satisfaction than finding the best solution to a problem. This could mean the best way to organize a team, the best way to present a product or idea, or even the best way to process inventory and execute services. Unfortunately, there is no such thing as a chief optimization officer. However, the thing about an obsession is that it is not optional. I’ll be an optimizer whether it’s my job title or not.

My drive to optimize is what lead me to get an M.S. in Physics from UT Dallas, where I am currently working on my PhD. I have been privileged to conduct research with Dr. David J. Lary and a diverse team of graduate, undergraduate, and high school students. The common theme throughout our research is utilizing tools from artificial intelligence, automation, and data science to promote scientific discovery and public service. Our projects span several scales and spaces, from building and calibrating low cost weather sensor networks, deploying a robotic water and aerial vehicle team, and studying environmental impacts on cognitive load and performance.

Outside my academic and professional projects, I am a connoisseur of various breads and bread affiliates. This is almost matched by my love of spending time with friends and family. In my downtime, I enjoy pursuing creative endeavors such as making music, writing, and painting.

StrengthsQuest: Achiever, Learner, Input, Intellection, Relator
